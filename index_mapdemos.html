Toponym Identification on Scanned Old Maps

This page showcases some experimental work on the automatic identification of toponyms on scanned old maps, using Open Source software. While fully automatic detection and transcription of place names is unrealistic based on today's technology, our goal is eliminate as much as possible of the heavy lifting and manual preparatory work required to annotate scanned maps.

This work is in an early stage & yet unfunded, so bear with us regarding the bare-bone state of this site!

Automatic Toponym Transcription

Once we have identified candidates that may be place names, we need to transcribe them. Automating the transcription is the most challenging step in the process; and it is highly dependent on the type of map whether there is even a chance to achieve this with present-day technology. We are currently experimenting with Gamera to get a feel for what can be achieved with state of the art tools.

Manual Annotation

While machines fail miserably in map transcription tasks, humans can read and transcribe place name labels with relative (depending on the type of map) ease. It is simply the magnitude of the task that makes it challenging to deal with: map collections often host 1.000s of maps, each potentially containing 100s of place names.

Our goal is to build tools that allow human users to perform selection, transcription, and annotation tasks as quickly as possible.
